Polluting friendships

The EU wants at least 32% participation of renewable sources in the 27-nation bloc’s electricity generation by 2030, but Poland’s plan aims at 23% participation of green energy, and still up to 56% participation of coal in its energy mix.

Coal makes up 65% of Poland’s energy sources, including 17% from lignite, while about 25% of the country’s energy comes from renewable sources and biofuels. Another 10% comes from gas and other sources. Poland’s continuing heavy reliance on coal is a source of tension in the EU, which is seeking to meet ambitious goals to reduce the bloc’s greenhouse gas emissions and stop global heating.
